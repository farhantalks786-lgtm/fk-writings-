After War

By Farhan | October 30, 2025

The war ends.

Leaders shake hands,

National embassies reopen,

And the world hails them as legends.

But somewhere,

An old widow still waits for her son —

He may have fallen, just like his father.

An orphaned child looks endlessly

Across the silent fields,

Searching for the face of a brave father

Who will never return.

A young woman waits for her husband,

Perhaps buried in an unknown yard.

Her name becomes widow,

Her children — orphans.

They do not know

What the war was for,

But they know —

They paid for it.
